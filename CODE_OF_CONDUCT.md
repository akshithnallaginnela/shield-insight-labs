# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free community for all participants in the ScamShield AI project, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Being respectful** - Use welcoming and inclusive language
- **Being considerate** - Think about how your words and actions affect others
- **Being collaborative** - Work constructively with others
- **Giving credit** - Acknowledge contributions from others
- **Being patient** - Help people learn and grow
- **Assuming good intent** - Interpret ambiguity in the most charitable way

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassing comments related to personal characteristics
- Deliberate intimidation or threats
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting comments, or personal attacks
- Publishing private information without consent
- Spam or excessive self-promotion
- Any form of discrimination or abuse

## Enforcement

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to behavior that violates this Code of Conduct.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject:
- Comments
- Commits
- Code
- Issues
- Pull requests
- Other contributions

...that do not align with this Code of Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- All project spaces (GitHub, issues, discussions, etc.)
- Official project events and communications
- Public spaces when representing the project

## Reporting Issues

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the project maintainers at **Akshith.nallaginnela@gmail.com**.

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and confidentially. The project team will determine appropriate corrective action.

## Confidentiality

Project maintainers are obligated to maintain confidentiality regarding the reporter of an incident.

## Consequences

Inappropriate behavior may result in:
1. Warning or temporary ban
2. Permanent removal from community
3. Legal action (for serious violations)
